## Understanding Contact Records

### What is a Contact Record?

A contact stores:
- Person's name and contact information
- Which management company they belong to
- Their category/role
- Whether they're a primary contact
- Any notes about the person

### Contacts vs. Other People Records

| Record Type | Purpose |
|-------------|---------|
| **Contacts** | Customer-side personnel |
| **Mechanics** | Your field technicians |
| **Inspectors** | External inspection officials |
| **Team** | Your app users |

## Primary Contacts

### What is a Primary Contact?

A primary contact is the main point of contact for a management company. When you need to reach someone at that company, start with the primary contact.

### Setting a Primary Contact

1. Open the contact detail page
2. Check the **"Is Primary"** checkbox
3. Save changes

### Multiple Primary Contacts

You can have multiple primary contacts per company if needed (e.g., one for operations, one for billing).

## Best Practices

### Complete Information

- Always include phone number for emergency contacts
- Add email for regular communications
- Include extension numbers if applicable

### Clear Notes

Document useful information:
- Best times to reach them
- Communication preferences
- Special instructions
- Their assistant's contact info

### Primary Contact Strategy

- Designate at least one primary per company
- Update when personnel changes
- Consider having backup contacts documented

### Category Consistency

- Use the same categories across companies
- Create categories that match common roles
- Avoid too many similar categories

### Regular Updates

- Verify contacts periodically
- Update when you hear of personnel changes
- Archive contacts who are no longer there
